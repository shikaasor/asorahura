Product Requirements Document (PRD)
asorahura.com — Solo Expert Repositioning
Version: 1.0 | Date: May 13, 2026 | Author: Site Audit & Strategy

1. EXECUTIVE SUMMARY
This PRD defines the complete rebuild of asorahura.com from a hybrid personal-brand / agency (Flowmorph) site into a high-conversion solo expert platform. The site's singular job is to convert CEOs, CTOs, and Operations Managers into paying clients — either by purchasing a productized service directly or booking a paid strategy call. Every design, copy, and UX decision must serve that conversion goal. Flowmorph is retired entirely. There is one expert: Asor Ahura.

2. CURRENT SITE AUDIT FINDINGS
2.1 Brand & Positioning Problems
Dual Identity Confusion. The homepage presents two competing brands simultaneously — "Asor Ahura" and "Flowmorph" — with two CTAs side by side ("Work With Me" and "Explore Flowmorph"). A prospect landing on the page must decide which entity they're engaging. This immediately creates cognitive friction and dilutes trust in either brand.
Wrong Audience Messaging. The hero copy ("AI Automation Made Simple") and the how-I-can-help section speak in technical language ("LangChain, RAG, LLMs, n8n, API integration"). This resonates with developers, not CEOs or CTOs. The target buyer doesn't care about the stack — they care about outcomes: time saved, headcount avoided, scale achieved.
No Pain-First Hook. The homepage opens with Asor's credentials and identity. The target buyer (an executive with a scaling problem) is not looking for a biography. They need to see their problem articulated back to them within the first 3 seconds of landing.
Weak Flowmorph Page. The Flowmorph page operates as a completely separate micro-site with its own navigation, branding, footer, and CTAs. It fragments the user journey and routes traffic away from the main conversion funnel. It is to be completely removed.
Engage Page Has High Friction. The contact/inquiry form asks for 8 fields including "Monthly Operational Volume" (a question most executives cannot answer off the top of their head) and budget ranges starting at $15k/month, which is disconnected from the new project-based pricing tiers. The form does not route prospects to outcomes — it just collects data into a void.
No Lead Magnet / Email Capture. There is no mechanism to capture emails from visitors who are not yet ready to buy. Traffic that bounces is lost permanently. There is no email list being built.
Case Studies Are Buried & Misframed. Impressive results (7,200+ hours automated, Swiss insurance NLP pipeline, resume screening platform) are presented on a secondary "Work" page with a header — "Intellectual Dominance through Systems" — that speaks to engineers, not operators or executives. The EU Horizon grant reference is being removed per direction.
Articles/Blog Has No Conversion Layer. The Articles page is a clean case study archive but has no CTA, no email capture, and no bridge to the AI Readiness Assessment or any offer. It functions as a dead end.
No Social Proof on Homepage. The three testimonials (25% efficiency gain, 7,200 hours saved, scalable growth) are buried on the Engage page inside the inquiry form — the last place a new visitor will see them.
Live Demos Are Underused. The AI Resume Review and Automation System Demo are listed in a small section called "Live Systems" with minimal context. These are powerful trust builders and should be showcased more prominently.
2.2 Navigation Problems
Current nav: Work | Articles | Flowmorph | Work with Me

"Flowmorph" is being retired
"Articles" needs to become "Blog" and serve as a content funnel back to the site
Navigation has no reference to the AI Readiness Assessment (the primary lead magnet)
The CTA button "Work with Me" is correct in intent but the destination page needs a complete overhaul

2.3 Conversion Flow Problems
There is currently no defined funnel. A visitor lands → reads credentials → optionally clicks "Work with Me" → fills out an 8-field form → waits. There is no:

Urgency or scarcity signal
Clear offer menu
Pricing anchor
Email capture on exit
Lead magnet to capture not-yet-ready buyers
Routing logic to the right offer based on problem/budget

For: CEOs, CTOs, and Operations Managers at scaling businesses

"Your business doesn't have a people problem. It has a systems problem. AI can handle the execution. Your team should be focused on the decisions, relationships, and creative work that machines can't replicate yet."

Direct, confident, operator-to-operator
No jargon unless immediately translated into business impact
No humility theater ("I help businesses...") — own the authority
Short sentences. Heavy use of specifics (numbers, hours, percentages)
